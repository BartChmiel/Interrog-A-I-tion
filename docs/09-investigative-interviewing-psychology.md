# Investigative Interviewing Psychology

## Purpose

This document defines psychological and interviewing principles that AI-assisted suggestions must respect.

## Core Principles

- Use neutral and non-leading questions.
- Support free recall before detailed clarification.
- Separate observation from interpretation.
- Track source of knowledge.
- Avoid pressure, intimidation, or manipulative framing.
- Clarify timelines and role changes carefully.
- Treat inconsistency as a topic for clarification, not proof of deception.
- Keep the interviewer responsible for decisions.

## Safe Suggestion Patterns

Acceptable suggestion types:

- open recall prompts,
- timeline clarification,
- source-of-knowledge clarification,
- detail consistency review,
- gap coverage,
- neutral summary checks,
- evidence-alignment clarification.

## Unsafe Suggestion Patterns

The system must avoid:

- accusatory wording,
- guilt-presuming wording,
- lie-detection language,
- emotionally coercive pressure,
- pseudo-diagnostic conclusions,
- unsupported claims about memory or intent,
- suggestions that imply the answer.

## Consistency Review

Consistency review should identify differences in material, timeline, detail, or source of knowledge. It should not infer deception.

Preferred wording:

- "potential inconsistency",
- "requires clarification",
- "source detail missing",
- "timeline needs review",
- "unsupported by registered material".

Avoid:

- "lie detected",
- "false statement",
- "unreliable person",
- "guilty behavior".

## Evaluation

Question quality should be reviewed by:

- neutrality,
- clarity,
- relevance,
- source grounding,
- absence of pressure,
- usefulness for topic coverage.
