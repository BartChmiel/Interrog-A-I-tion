# Interview Methodology and AI

## Methodological Position

AI assistance should support structured, neutral, and source-aware interviewing. It must not steer the interviewer toward coercive, suggestive, or verdict-oriented behavior.

## Supported Interviewing Principles

- Prepare topics and objectives before the interview.
- Use open and neutral questions where possible.
- Allow free narrative before detailed clarification.
- Distinguish observation, inference, hearsay, and uncertainty.
- Track source of knowledge.
- Clarify timelines and role changes carefully.
- Avoid pressure-oriented wording.
- Keep the interviewer responsible for decisions.

## AI Tasks

AI may assist with:

- interview plan drafting,
- neutral follow-up suggestions,
- topic gap detection,
- potential inconsistency surfacing,
- summary drafting,
- question wording review,
- source-linked evidence alignment.

## AI Boundaries

AI must not:

- decide truthfulness,
- diagnose psychological state,
- label a person as lying,
- infer guilt,
- recommend coercive tactics,
- generate hidden official-record changes,
- produce unsupported claims without traceable source context.

## Cross-Validation Questions

The system may suggest asking about the same topic through different neutral formulations. The purpose is to clarify consistency and detail, not to trap or manipulate the interviewee.

Suggestions should be framed as:

- clarification,
- timeline ordering,
- source-of-knowledge review,
- detail verification,
- contradiction resolution.

## Human Control

Every AI suggestion remains advisory. The operator can accept, edit, reject, or ignore it. Those decisions should be auditable.
