# Product Strategy

## Current Strategic Decisions

This document captures the product decisions made after the first strategy-question round.

## Target Institution

Primary target:

- police,
- prosecution.

Secondary future users:

- experts,
- training units,
- auditors,
- possibly courts or defense-side review workflows if legally appropriate.

## Product Ambition

The project should combine:

- master thesis work,
- real product architecture,
- ML/cybersecurity research,
- investigative interviewing psychology,
- institutional-grade security.

The goal is not a throwaway academic demo. The thesis prototype should be the first credible technical slice of a future tool.

## Product Character

The product should be a strong investigative intelligence tool, but one that respects:

- legal requirements,
- ethical constraints,
- social trust,
- human oversight,
- auditability,
- data protection.

It should not present itself as a confession tool, a lie detector, or an automated judge.

## Live-First Workflow

The product is live-interview-first.

Offline evaluation remains essential for research and testing, but the intended product value is strongest during an interview:

- preparing the plan,
- tracking questions,
- noting answers,
- showing visual bullet summaries,
- tracking coverage,
- identifying low consistency,
- proposing active assistant actions.

## Supported Roles

The system must support:

- witnesses,
- suspects,
- injured parties or victims,
- experts,
- other participants.

Role changes must be allowed. A person may move between procedural roles as the case develops.

## UI Direction

The UI should be:

- user-friendly,
- professional,
- clear,
- aesthetically polished,
- not confusing,
- suitable for long operational work.

It should not be a flashy landing page. It should feel like a serious operational tool.

Expected core live view:

- questions and topics on the left,
- answer notes in the center,
- live indicators and assistant suggestions on the right,
- summary/report/audit available without disrupting the interview.

## Language Direction

Engineering remains English-first.

User-facing language packs start with:

- Polish,
- English.

The first serious market path is:

1. Poland,
2. European Union,
3. global expansion.

## Thesis Direction

The thesis sits in computer science with an ML/cybersecurity specialization.

The strongest thesis framing is:

> A local, air-gapped AI system for live investigative interviewing support with auditable credibility indicators and secure case workspaces.

Relevant research dimensions:

- local LLMs,
- prompt and output validation,
- human-in-the-loop AI,
- high-risk AI governance,
- audit logs,
- secure local storage,
- evaluation of AI-generated investigative support,
- multilingual user-facing systems.
